About the Makati Human Milk Bank

The Makati Human Milk Bank (MHMB) provides safe, pasteurized donor milk to premature and medically fragile infants who cannot receive their mother's own milk.

The HMBMS replaces manual logbook processes with a fully digital system covering every stage — from donor screening and QR-coded milk collection to pasteurization logging, inventory management, and chain-of-custody tracking.

Every process is designed to comply with the DOH Philippine Human Milk Banking Guidelines and to protect sensitive health information under the Data Privacy Act of 2012.

The system is built to be accessible to Barangay Health Workers, nurses, medical technologists, and community mothers — ensuring that even users with varying levels of digital literacy can navigate it with ease.

How It Works

From the first donor health screening to the moment safe milk reaches a fragile infant — every step is digitally tracked and verified.

1

Donor Screening

Prospective donors complete a digital health questionnaire. Blood tests are conducted for HIV, Hepatitis B, and Syphilis. The system automatically excludes any donor testing positive for HIV or Hepatitis B.

Health & Safety First
2

Milk Collection

Enrolled donors express milk through our three programs. Each container is labeled with a unique QR code bearing donor name, date, time, and volume. Milk is stored immediately at −20°C.

QR Labeled & Cold-Stored
3

Pasteurization & Testing

Milk is pasteurized at exactly 62.5°C for 30 minutes following the Holder Pasteurization method. Lab tests are conducted before and after pasteurization. Only PASS batches are cleared for dispensing.

62.5°C · 30 min
4

Safe Dispensing

Recipients submit a doctor's prescription through the portal. The system applies FIFO logic to select the oldest safe batch. A complete digital chain of custody is maintained from collection to delivery.

Prescription-Based

Collection Programs

The MHMB runs three dedicated programs to reach donors where they are — in the community, at hospitals, and at home.

🤝

Supsup Todo

Community-Based

A mobile community outreach program that brings milk collection directly to barangay health centers and community sites. Designed for donors who cannot travel to the main facility. Barangay Health Workers log collection data via mobile device.

Learn more →
🏥

Milky Way

Hospital-Based

A hospital-based program serving premature babies confined in partner hospital NICUs. Milk is collected from hospital donors and delivered directly within the clinical setting, ensuring the shortest cold chain possible for the most vulnerable infants.

Learn more →
🏠

Mom's Act

Household-Based

A convenient household-based pickup service for pre-screened and enrolled donors. Scheduled pickups are coordinated through the HMBMS scheduling calendar, allowing donors to contribute from the comfort of their homes without disrupting their routines.
